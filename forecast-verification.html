Forecast Verification

Statistical analysis of forecast accuracy over time

Forecast verification is a crucial process to assess the accuracy and reliability of weather forecasts. NCHM uses a range of verification metrics and methods to evaluate the performance of our forecasting models and forecasters. This helps us identify areas for improvement and provide users with accurate information on forecast uncertainty.

Our verification process includes comparing forecasts against observed data from weather stations, satellite imagery, and radar. We analyze various forecast parameters such as temperature, precipitation, wind speed, and atmospheric pressure to provide a comprehensive assessment of forecast performance.

Metric Description Typical Values
Mean Error (ME) Average difference between forecasts and observations ±0.5°C (temperature)
Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) Square root of the average squared differences 1.2-2.5°C (temperature)
Threat Score (TS) Measure of precipitation forecast accuracy 0.3-0.6 (precipitation)
Probability of Detection (POD) Fraction of observed events correctly forecast 0.6-0.8 (precipitation)
